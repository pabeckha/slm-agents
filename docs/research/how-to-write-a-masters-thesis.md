# How to Write a Master's Thesis

Source: https://sunelehmann.com/how-to-write-a-masters-thesis/

## Overview

This guide from Sune Lehmann provides comprehensive advice for writing an excellent Master's thesis, emphasizing that "the point of your thesis is to **report your research process**" with accuracy and precision so others could reproduce the work.

## What Makes a Good Thesis

A strong thesis demonstrates:
- Your capabilities and learning
- Ability to apply knowledge to solve problems
- Potentially new knowledge generation

## 10 Key Tips

**1. Reproducibility**: Document methods accurately so someone else could replicate your work. Justify all choices made.

**2. Take Notes**: Record findings, parameters, and important results throughout research. The process matters more than just final results, making negative outcomes valuable.

**3. Reference Lists**: Aim for 20-50 quality references showing awareness of the research landscape. Identify central papers by citation count and expand through cited references and reverse citations.

**4. Research is Messy**: The scientific process is non-linear and confusing—this is normal and acceptable.

**5. Structure**: Follow a narrative format: Introduction → Literature Review → Data (if applicable) → Analysis Chapters → Conclusion. Each section should introduce, explain, then summarize content.

**6. Academic Writing Structure**: "First you tell your readers what you're about to tell them. Then you tell the readers the thing you want to tell them. Finally you tell them what you've just told them."

**7. Figure Captions**: Descriptions should explain what readers see and what they should notice—not just label the figure.

**8. Active Voice**: Use active rather than passive voice for clarity.

**9. Formal Tone**: Maintain professional language throughout (avoid contractions, use formal phrasing).

**10. Tools**: Use LaTeX for document formatting and BibTeX for bibliography management, especially for documents exceeding 40 pages.

## Evaluation Criteria

Examiners assess theses across five dimensions:

- **Scope & Goals**: Clearly define research boundaries early; revisit after completion
- **Topic Command**: Critically select relevant, high-quality sources; show understanding of broader context
- **Methods**: Justify technique choices; provide sufficient detail for reproduction
- **Conclusions**: Ensure reasoning is clear, accurate, and well-supported
- **Presentation**: Maintain impeccable formatting, spelling, and professional appearance

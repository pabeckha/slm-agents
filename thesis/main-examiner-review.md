# Examiner Review - Agents with Small Language Models

**Author:** Paulo Ricardo Beckhauser de Araujo · **Reviewed:** 2026-06-16 · **Pages:** 66 PDF pages / 57 report pages before back cover
**Reviewer note:** AI-assisted examiner review - to be verified and owned by the examiner.

## 1. Summary of the thesis

This thesis evaluates whether small language models, primarily Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, can be optimized for reliable tool calling without relying on frontier-model APIs. It studies constrained decoding, quantization, prompting, RAG, LoRA fine-tuning, schema-enriched prompts, and model-size scaling on BFCL v4 and selected tau-bench retail runs. The stated research questions are: "RQ1 What is the performance gap between unoptimized small language models (0.5B-7B parameters) and frontier models on tool-calling benchmarks?", "RQ2 What is the marginal contribution of each optimization technique (constrained decoding, prompt engineering, retrieval-augmented generation, quantization, and LoRA fine-tuning) when applied cumulatively to small models for tool calling?", and "RQ3 Can a fully optimized small language model configuration achieve production-viable tool-calling accuracy on consumer hardware?" (PDF pp. 11-12). The main conclusion is that constrained decoding solves format compliance, schema-enriched constrained decoding reaches 89.0% on BFCL simple python, but the evaluated approach does not transfer to multi-turn or repeated-call agentic settings (PDF pp. 56-60).

## 2. Overall assessment

This is a strong applied systems thesis with a clear problem, a well-scoped experimental ladder, unusually concrete failure analysis, and honest discussion of several negative results. The work is strongest where it stays within its actual evidence: single-call, schema-constrained BFCL tool calling under a Qwen2.5 model family. The central empirical finding - that schema-enriched constrained decoding closes much of the residual gap after plain constrained decoding - is useful, well quantified, and likely defensible. The main weaknesses are scope and claim calibration: "production viability" and "frontier range/parity" are sometimes stated more strongly than the evidence warrants because the cascade router is not implemented, frontier scores are not locally re-run, the strongest 89.0% result is confined to the easiest BFCL category at 7B, and multi-step/parallel results expose hard architectural boundaries. Based on the written thesis alone, the indicative DTU grade band is **10 (B), possibly 12 (A-) if the examiner accepts the scope as deliberately single-call and the code/results verify cleanly**. It would move down toward **7** if the experimental repository cannot reproduce the headline results or if the frontier/BFCL version comparison turns out not to be like-for-like; it would move up if the final version further tightens the production-viability claims and includes stronger evidence for CD+schema beyond simple python.

## 3. Strengths

- The research questions are explicit and operationalized with concrete criteria for frontier parity and cascade viability rather than left as vague "better tool use" claims (PDF p. 12).
- The experimental design is systematic: a cumulative ladder plus isolation runs makes it possible to distinguish constrained decoding from prompting, retrieval, quantization, and fine-tuning effects (PDF pp. 23-24, 35-36).
- The baseline is carefully bounded by the B-template control: the thesis correctly shows that the 1.5% model-agnostic baseline is an integration failure, not an intrinsic Qwen2.5-7B capability ceiling (PDF pp. 26-27, 30).
- The schema-enrichment result is the most persuasive contribution: CD+schema improves from roughly 72.5% to 89.0%, with a paired McNemar table showing 73 improvements against 7 regressions (PDF pp. 32-33).
- Negative results are not buried. Few-shot prompting, chain-of-thought, RAG, misaligned LoRA, tau-bench, and same-function parallel calling are all reported and analyzed with plausible mechanisms (PDF pp. 31-37, 42-46).
- The limitations chapter is unusually candid, especially about BFCL simple python being the least demanding category, the unimplemented cascade, single-family model scope, and the tau-bench simulator mismatch (PDF pp. 50-53).
- The AI disclosure is clear and assigns responsibility to the author for results, claims, and citations (PDF p. 63).

## 4. Evaluation by dimension

**Methodology & rigor.** The methodology is fit for RQ1 and RQ2 within a single-call BFCL setting: model versions, hardware, vLLM version, temperature, benchmark categories, sample sizes, and paired tests are described in enough detail to be reproducible in principle (PDF pp. 23-29, 32, 38-39). The thesis also reports run-to-run spread and interprets small deltas conservatively, for example the 0.5 pp CD-to-CD+Q difference and the borderline fine-tuning gain (PDF pp. 24, 39, 52). The rigor weakens for RQ3 because production viability is inferred from benchmark accuracy and memory footprint rather than measured in an implemented cascade with routing, workload distribution, failure detection, and real deployment cost (PDF pp. 12, 52-54).

**Technical/mathematical correctness.** The technical description of constrained decoding, quantization, LoRA, the two-stage pipeline, and benchmark metrics is coherent and mostly aligned with the reported experiments (PDF pp. 16-22, 26-29). Statistical handling is generally sound: Wilson intervals, McNemar tests, binary tau-bench standard errors, and run-to-run spread are all discussed (PDF pp. 32, 38-39, 41-43). I could not independently verify the stored predictions, code, BFCL data version, or leaderboard extraction from the thesis text alone; those are important verification points because several headline conclusions depend on exact per-case pairing and category matching.

**Contribution & novelty.** The thesis is not proposing a new algorithm, but it makes a credible applied contribution by isolating the practical value of schema-enriched constrained decoding and by showing that generic prompting/RAG interventions can hurt once structural validity is enforced (PDF pp. 32-37, 48-50). The contribution is strongest as a careful ablation and deployment-oriented measurement study. Novelty is limited by the use of known techniques and a single model family, but the combination, failure taxonomy, and cost-benefit ordering are useful at MSc level.

**Business/practical relevance.** The practical framing is clear: local SLMs are motivated by offline, privacy-sensitive, and high-volume cost-constrained deployments, and the thesis translates accuracy deltas into cascade cost implications (PDF pp. 9-10, 52-54). The business relevance remains partly speculative because no real workload, router, acceptance criterion, semantic confidence mechanism, API-cost sensitivity analysis beyond a simple formula, or production tool catalog is evaluated. The thesis acknowledges this, but the RQ3 conclusion should be phrased more as "single-call cascade candidate" than "production-viable tool-calling system" (PDF pp. 52-59).

## 6. Questions for the defense

1. Your best constrained result, CD+schema, is 89.0% on BFCL simple python. What do you expect its score to be on the multiple and parallel multiple categories, and why?
2. If constrained decoding makes every output structurally valid, what concrete signal would your cascade router use to detect semantic argument errors before executing or accepting the call?
3. The B-template control reaches 96.00%, well above CD+schema. In a single-model deployment, why would a team choose the model-agnostic constrained pipeline over the native template?
4. The parallel category fails by construction because the pipeline emits at most one call per distinct function. How would you redesign the output schema and decoding process to support repeated calls to the same function?
5. Your RAG setup has 97.2% recall@5 but poor final accuracy. Would lowering k, reranking, or training on retrieved candidate sets be the first change you would test, and what failure mode would each address?
6. Which of your conclusions do you expect to transfer to Llama 3.2 or Phi-4, and which are likely specific to Qwen2.5's tool-use training and native template?

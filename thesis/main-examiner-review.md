## 1. Summary of the thesis

The thesis asks which optimization techniques make a small language model (Qwen 2.5, 0.5B–7B) reliable enough for tool calling, and what each technique costs or buys in accuracy. It runs a cumulative ablation — constrained decoding (CD), schema enrichment, AWQ INT4 quantization, few-shot prompting, chain-of-thought, RAG, and LoRA fine-tuning — primarily on BFCL v4 `simple python` (400 cases), with extensions to harder BFCL categories, τ-bench retail (multi-turn), and a live GitHub MCP tool-selection test. The three research questions (verbatim) are: **RQ1** the gap between unoptimized SLMs and frontier models; **RQ2** the marginal contribution of each technique applied cumulatively; **RQ3** whether an optimized config reaches "cascade-viable" single-call accuracy on consumer hardware. Headline findings: CD removes the dominant format-compliance failure (1.5% → 72.75% at 7B); schema-enriched CD reaches 89.0% with no training; format-aligned LoRA reaches 76.75%; and single-call gains do **not** transfer to multi-turn agency (τ-bench 4.35%).

## 2. Overall assessment

This is a strong, honest, empirically careful MSc thesis that is held back by incremental novelty and one mechanical-but-must-fix internal inconsistency. The experimental design is the right one for the questions: a cumulative ladder plus an isolation arm that cleanly separates each technique's standalone effect from constrained decoding's contribution. The statistical hygiene is above the MSc norm — Wilson confidence intervals, paired McNemar tests on stored per-case predictions, an explicitly documented ±0.5 pp run-to-run spread, and a threats-to-validity section that names the *real* limitations (oracle function provision, single model family, a 7B τ-bench user simulator, accuracy-as-routing-proxy) rather than safe ones. The schema-enrichment result (CD+schema 89.0%, McNemar p<0.00001, b=73/c=7) and the format-alignment finding (two adapters, near-identical training loss, 7.0 pp apart purely from output format) are genuine, well-tested, level-appropriate contributions.

Two things hold it back. First, the contribution is an *application* of known techniques (CD, AWQ, LoRA, RAG) to a known model on a known benchmark; the novelty lives in the systematic combination and two specific findings, which is fine for an MSc but caps the ceiling. Second — and this is the central tension the student must own at the defense — the thesis's most dramatic framing (a "96 pp" gap, a "1.5%" floor, "+71 pp from CD") is an artifact of a deliberately weakened model-agnostic baseline, and the thesis's *own* honest numbers walk it back: the B-template control shows the same model reaches 96.00% out of the box with its native template, the lenient-parser base is 62.00%, and CD's true marginal contribution is only +10.75 pp. The thesis discloses all of this, repeatedly and to its credit, but the abstract still leads with the inflated framing. The work is at its best when it says the quiet part: CD's real value is the *structural guarantee*, not raw accuracy.

**Indicative grade band (written thesis only): 10 (B), with a credible path to 10–12.** The rigor, honesty, and breadth of evaluation sit comfortably at 10. What would move it toward **12**: fixing the stale Appendix C table (trivial), realigning the abstract's emphasis with the body's honest framing, and either strengthening the novelty claim or sharpening the multi-turn boundary into a more developed result. What would pull it toward **7**: leaving the Appendix C contradiction in (a censor will hit it first), or failing to defend the baseline-design choice convincingly. The band is derived from the issue severities in §5 — one Major correctness inconsistency (mechanically fixable), a Major positioning issue, and several Minor items — against otherwise strong, complete work.

## 3. Strengths

- **Clean experimental design separating two confounded effects.** The isolation arm (PE-ng, ITC-ng, RAG-ng, no guided decoding) lets the thesis quantify CD's contribution *on top of* each technique, not just in aggregate — this is what produces the honest +10.75 pp figure (Table 4.6, p.30) rather than the misleading +71 pp.
- **Statistical discipline rare at MSc level.** Paired McNemar on stored predictions (Tables 4.2, p.26; §4.4, p.31), Wilson CIs (~±4.1 pp at n=400, p.33), and an explicitly characterized run-to-run spread used to *downgrade* the student's own borderline result (CD+FT-aligned p=0.044 called "borderline rather than established", p.31). The thesis argues against its own significance where warranted.
- **The schema-enrichment finding is the real contribution.** CD+schema 89.0% (+16.25 pp over CD, McNemar p<0.00001; §4.2.3, p.25) is a clean, no-training result with a convincing mechanism (the generic pipeline discards parameter descriptions/enums/defaults that the native template and frontier APIs carry).
- **The format-alignment result is well-controlled.** Two LoRA adapters, identical hyperparameters, near-identical final loss (0.203 vs 0.229), 7.0 pp apart in BFCL accuracy purely from output format (Fig. 4.4, p.31) — a tidy demonstration that extends Gorilla's data-relevance insight to format alignment.
- **Honest negative results.** Few-shot (−2.5 pp at 7B), CoT (−6.75 pp, with a four-way flip analysis and verbatim reasoning traces, Fig. 4.2/Table 4.3, p.26–27), and RAG (−24.5 pp despite recall@5 of 97.2%, with disambiguation diagnosed as the cause) are reported and mechanistically explained rather than buried.
- **Scope honesty on the multi-turn boundary.** The 68.4 pp BFCL→τ-bench gap (§4.6, p.35) is presented as the limit of the techniques studied, and RQ3 is explicitly scoped to single-call tool calling.
- **Reproducibility.** Public repo, hyperparameter appendix (Table B.1, p.60), HPC/software versions, seeds, and a generative-AI disclosure (Appendix A) consistent with DTU policy.

## 4. Evaluation by dimension

**Methodology & rigor.** Fit-for-purpose and unusually self-critical. The two-arm ablation is the correct design, temperature-0 determinism removes a confound, and the threats-to-validity section (§5.3.1, p.47–49) is candid about oracle function provision, the single Qwen family, the 7B τ-bench simulator (which deflates pass rates), and accuracy-as-routing-proxy (p values explicitly called upper bounds). The one genuine methodological soft spot the student already flags: the three PE few-shot examples were authored from *test-split* error inspection (p.20, restated p.48) — biasing PE in its own favour, which makes its failure conservative rather than inflated. Good that it is disclosed; a held-out dev split would have been the clean design. The parallel "0% by construction" (§4.9.2, p.40) is a fixed pipeline limitation honestly characterized, but listing it as contribution #4 (p.5) overstates it — it is a harness boundary, not a finding about model capability.

**Technical / mathematical correctness.** Spot-checked headline numbers reconcile: τ-bench mean 4.35% = 5/115 ✓; latency 6.995→0.878 s (8×) ✓; AWQ 14.25→5.20 GiB (63.5%) ✓; McNemar b=73/c=7 with the p<0.00001 claim plausible ✓; Wilson CI ~±4.1 pp at n=400, p≈0.75 ✓. The single substantive correctness defect is the **Appendix C Table C.1 inconsistency** (see §5, #1): five no-guided rows (RAG-ng, PE-ng, ITC-ng, FT-only, FT-aligned-ng) carry stale strict-parser counts that contradict the body's lenient-parser numbers by 30–50 pp. I verified against the stored source-of-truth `scores/` files: the **body is correct** (RAG-ng 208/400=52.0%, PE-ng 236/400=59.0%, FT-only 212/400=53.0%, FT-aligned-ng 164/400=41.0%); Appendix C predates the parser correction. This is the same parser-sensitivity issue the thesis itself discusses for the baseline — it just was not propagated to the appendix.

**Contribution & novelty.** Incremental but legitimate. No new method, model, or benchmark; the contribution is (a) a systematic cumulative+isolation ablation across four sizes, (b) the schema-enrichment result, and (c) the format-alignment result. Related work is adequate and fairly represented (Willard & Louf, Gorilla, xLAM/APIGen, ToolLLM, τ-bench, AWQ, the SLM-agentic survey). For 35 ECTS this is appropriate scope; it is not a publishable research breakthrough, and the thesis does not claim to be.

**Business / practical relevance.** The cascade framing is the applied hook, and the thesis is admirably explicit that **no router or cascade is implemented** — the economics (§5.4, p.49) are a projection. The break-even model (N = C_train/(Δp·c_frontier) ≈ 15,000 queries) rests on illustrative figures ($0.01/call, $3 training) and an *ideal semantic router* that does not exist post-CD (structural routing signals vanish once every output is valid JSON). This is disclosed but it means RQ3's "cascade-viable" answer is really "single-model accuracy clears a chosen threshold," not "the cascade works." The practical guideline that most undercuts the thesis's own framing — *for a single frozen Qwen deployment, just use the native template (96%)* — is stated plainly (Step 1, p.54), which is honest but invites the obvious defense question.

## 6. Questions for the defense

1. **The baseline question.** Your B-template control shows the same 7B model reaches 96.00% out of the box with its native template, above every constrained configuration. Your lenient-parser base is 62%, making CD's marginal contribution +10.75 pp, not +71 pp. So for a real Qwen deployment, why would a team use your constrained pipeline rather than the native template — and does the "96 pp gap" headline survive that?
2. **Appendix C.** Table C.1 reports RAG-ng at 2.5% and ITC-ng at 26.25%; Chapter 4 reports 52.0% and 56.25%. Which is correct, why do they differ, and what does the discrepancy imply about the +10.75 pp vs +70 pp reading of CD's value?
3. **CD+schema vs the control.** CD+schema (89%) reaches frontier range by adding schema detail that the native template and frontier APIs already carry. Is the 89% a property of constrained decoding, or simply of restoring information your own pipeline discarded? What does CD contribute *over* a schema-rich native-template prompt?
4. **Cascade viability.** RQ3 answers "yes," but no router exists and you show structural routing signals vanish after CD. On what evidence is the cascade *viable* rather than merely *accurate enough in isolation*, and what would the residual-semantic-error router actually key on?
5. **Multi-turn.** τ-bench is 4.35% and flat across sizes — but with a 7B user simulator that you say deflates the score. How much of the 68.4 pp gap is agent incapacity versus simulator noise, and what would a frontier simulator change?
6. **Generality.** Everything load-bearing is Qwen 2.5. The cross-family checks (Phi-4, Llama, Gemma) are limited to no-guided collapse and MCP selection. Which of your directional findings (technique ordering, PE reversal at 7B, quantization profile) would you bet transfer, and which not?

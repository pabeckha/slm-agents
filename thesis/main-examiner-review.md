## 1. Summary of the thesis

The thesis asks which inference-time and training-time optimization techniques make a small language model (Qwen 2.5, 0.5B–7B) reliably call tools, and at what accuracy cost. It runs a cumulative ablation on BFCL v4 (primarily `simple_python`, 400 cases) plus τ-bench retail, layering constrained decoding (CD), AWQ INT4 quantization, few-shot prompting, chain-of-thought, RAG, LoRA fine-tuning, and schema-enriched prompting. Three RQs: (RQ1) the unoptimized SLM–frontier gap; (RQ2) the marginal contribution of each technique; (RQ3) whether an optimized configuration is production-viable on consumer hardware. Headline claims: the unoptimized baseline (1.5%) failure is format-compliance, not capability (confirmed by a 96.00% native-template control); CD alone recovers +71.25 pp to 72.75%; schema-enriched CD reaches 89.0% and enters the lower frontier band; single-call gains do not transfer to multi-turn (τ-bench 4.35%) or parallel (0%).

## 2. Overall assessment

This is a strong, genuinely well-executed empirical thesis. Its defining quality is methodological honesty: the **B-template control (96.00%)** is an excellent piece of design that converts a potentially naive "SLMs can't tool-call" story into a precise claim about *integration* vs *capability*; the statistical treatment (Wilson CIs, paired McNemar on stored per-case files, an explicitly documented ±0.5 pp run-to-run spread that the author refuses to over-read) is well above the median Master's thesis; and the negative results (few-shot, CoT, RAG all regress) are reported and mechanistically explained rather than buried. The writing is clear and the argument is disciplined.

What holds it back from the top band is concentrated in a few load-bearing places. First, the **central RQ1 number (1.5%) is parser-dependent** in a way the thesis does not disclose — the project's own records show a later, more lenient JSON extractor materially changes the unoptimized baseline, which puts the "+71.25 pp format-compliance" framing at risk (see §5, issue 1). Second, the **headline 89.0% (CD+schema) rests on a single configuration at a single size (7B)** with no size sweep and no isolation arm. Third, the **parallel "architectural boundary" claim is overstated and internally inconsistent** — and is in fact fixable at inference time (the author's own limitations section describes the fix). Fourth, the **τ-bench 4.35%** carries an unquantified downward bias from using a 7B model as its own user simulator. None of these is fatal; all are fixable, and three of the four are already self-identified somewhere in the text.

**Indicative grade band (written report only): 10 (B) — very good, with a few real but non-fatal weaknesses.** This is advisory. The single thing that could pull it toward **7 (C)** is issue 1 if the parser-dependence proves real and goes undisclosed, because it touches the central RQ1 claim. The things that would move it toward **12 (A)** are: disclosing/justifying the baseline parser (or reporting a lenient-parser baseline), size-sweeping or scope-limiting CD+schema, correcting the parallel claim, and reconciling the RQ2 wording with the techniques actually evaluated.

## 3. Strengths

- **The B-template control (p18, p22, Table 4.1).** Prompting the same model through its native template (96.00%, no parse failures) cleanly separates integration quality from model capability and anchors every downstream interpretation. This is the methodological backbone of the thesis and it is genuinely good.
- **Honest, mechanistic negative results.** Few-shot (−2.5 pp, p23), CoT (−6.75 pp, with a per-case flip analysis and verbatim reasoning traces, p25–26), and RAG (−24.5 pp with recall@5 = 97.2%, isolating disambiguation as the bottleneck, p26–27) are all reported and explained rather than hidden.
- **Statistical discipline (p16, p30–31, §5.3.1).** Paired McNemar on stored predictions, Wilson CIs, and an explicit run-to-run spread that the author uses to *down*-weight his own borderline results (e.g. treating the p=0.044 FT gain as "borderline rather than established").
- **The format-alignment LoRA result (p29–30).** Two adapters, identical hyperparameters and near-identical loss, 7.0 pp apart on BFCL solely due to output-format alignment — a clean, well-isolated finding that extends Gorilla's data-relevance insight.
- **Schema-enrichment finding (§4.2.3).** +16.5 pp with an internally consistent McNemar table (b=73, c=7; 283/37/73/7 sum to 400; CD=290, CD+schema=356 both check out) and a convincing "information loss, not capability" interpretation.
- **Scope honesty.** §5.3 limitations and §5.3.1 threats-to-validity are unusually candid (single family, oracle function provision, cross-harness frontier scores, accuracy-as-routing-proxy).

## 4. Evaluation by dimension

**Methodology & rigor.** Strong. The cumulative ladder + isolation arm is a sound design, the controls are well-chosen, and the statistics are honest. The main rigor gaps are: (a) the undisclosed parser-dependence of the baseline (issue 1); (b) the headline CD+schema config is unswept across size and never tested in an isolation (`schema-ng`) arm, so "schema info" and "schema info × CD" are not separated, though the unchanged-FSM argument partly covers this; (c) the PE few-shot examples are admittedly informed by the *same* test split they're evaluated on (p20) — well-flagged, and since PE is a negative result the contamination is conservative, but a held-out dev set is the clean design.

**Technical / mathematical correctness.** The numbers I could check are internally consistent: CD 291/400 = 72.75%; CD+schema 356/400 = 89.0%; the McNemar contingency sums correctly; AWQ memory 14.25→5.20 GiB (63.5%) is arithmetically right. The cost model (N = C_train/(Δp·c_frontier), break-even ≈ 15,000 queries, p51) is sound given its inputs. One presentational wrinkle: the paired baseline (CD = 290/400, +16.5 pp) and the headline CD (291/400, +16.25 pp) differ by one case; the author reconciles this explicitly, but it recurs and reads as a slight inconsistency. The Transformer/scaling/AWQ background math is correct and appropriately scoped.

**Contribution & novelty.** Solid for a Master's: a systematic, same-family, multi-size ablation that the cited SLM survey explicitly flags as underexplored, plus two clean isolated findings (format-alignment; schema-enrichment as the dominant residual component). The contribution is consolidation-and-isolation rather than a new method — appropriate at this level. Contribution 4 (the parallel "boundary") is the weakest and is overstated (issue 3).

**Business / practical relevance.** Above average. The cascade framing is concrete, the cost model is explicit with named assumptions, and §5.4 honestly concedes that for a single frozen model the native template (96%) dominates the constrained configs — a point a weaker thesis would have hidden because it undercuts the headline. The relevance claim is appropriately hedged: no cascade router is built, and p-values are upper bounds assuming an ideal semantic router (stated, p44).

## 6. Questions for the defense

1. **The 1.5% baseline:** what JSON-extraction rule produced it, and what does the *same* unoptimized pipeline score under a lenient first-object parser? If a reasonable generic integration would extract the first JSON object, is "+71.25 pp" the right framing of CD's contribution, or is it partly a parser artifact?
2. **CD+schema is your headline (89%, "enters the frontier range") but is 7B-only.** What is it at 0.5B–3B, and how do you know schema enrichment isn't interacting with scale the way few-shot does (which reversed sign at 7B)?
3. **Parallel:** your §5.3 says the fix is a JSON-array-of-call-objects schema, but §4.8.4 says it "would require training data." Which is it — and if the schema change suffices, is "0% is the architecture's ceiling" still a defensible contribution?
4. **τ-bench:** with a 7B model simulating the user, how much of the 4.35% is the agent and how much is the simulator? What is the claim if you can't separate them?
5. **B-template reaches 96% and you concede it dominates the constrained configs for a single frozen model (§5.4).** What, concretely, is the deployment scenario where the constrained pipeline's structural guarantee is worth ~7–24 pp of accuracy?
6. **RQ2 enumerates five techniques; you evaluate seven configurations' worth.** Which techniques is RQ2 actually answering, and why are chain-of-thought and schema enrichment absent from its wording?

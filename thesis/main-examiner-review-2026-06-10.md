# Examiner Review (re-review): Agents with Small Language Models — Optimization Techniques for Reliable Reasoning and Tool Calling

**Author:** Paulo Ricardo Beckhauser de Araujo · **Reviewed:** 2026-06-10 · **Pages:** 62 (PDF), ~53 printed
**Reviewer note:** AI-assisted examiner review, to be verified and owned by the examiner. This is a second review; the first (2026-06-09, `main-examiner-review.md`) found 2 Critical and 6 Major issues. Page numbers below are printed page numbers (PDF page minus 8).

## 1. Summary of the thesis

The thesis asks which optimization techniques make a small language model (Qwen 2.5, 0.5B–7B) reliable enough at tool calling to serve as the local tier of a cascade architecture. Three research questions are stated (p. 3): RQ1, the performance gap between unoptimized SLMs and frontier models; RQ2, the marginal contribution of each technique (constrained decoding, prompt engineering, RAG, quantization, LoRA) applied cumulatively; RQ3, whether a fully optimized SLM configuration achieves production-viable tool-calling accuracy on consumer hardware, with viability now explicitly defined (single-call accuracy at or above the lowest frontier score in the comparison set, on a 24 GiB consumer GPU). An eleven-configuration ablation on BFCL v4, plus isolation controls, a native-template control (B-template, 96.00%), extended BFCL categories, and τ-bench retail, concludes that the unoptimized gap is an integration-quality gap, that constrained decoding lifts the 7B model from 1.5% to 72.75%, that format-aligned LoRA reaches 76.75% (McNemar p = 0.044, reported as borderline), and that single-call gains do not transfer to multi-turn tasks (4.35% on τ-bench).

## 2. Overall assessment

The revision has resolved both Critical issues from the first review, and resolved them well. The B-template control (96.00%, pp. 17–18, Table 4.1 p. 21) does exactly what the baseline-fairness objection demanded: it bounds the model's native capability, reframes RQ1 as measuring integration cost rather than model deficit (abstract, §5.1 p. 36, §6.1 p. 44), and is then used honestly throughout, including against the thesis's own frontier-parity claims (§4.5 p. 31). The parallel-category contradiction is gone: §3.5 (p. 18) now describes the parallel selection stage precisely, and the 0% result is correctly derived from the pipeline's one-call-per-distinct-function structure (§5.3 p. 39), consistent with the multi-call outputs observed on parallel multiple. The previously missing significance tests now exist (McNemar, §4.4.1 p. 28 and §4.4.2 pp. 28–29) and are interpreted with unusual restraint: the headline +4.0 pp gain is explicitly downgraded to "borderline" against the measured run-to-run spread, and the determinism claim that the spread falsified was corrected rather than quietly dropped (§3.2.1 p. 15). The threats-to-validity subsection (§5.3.1 pp. 40–41) now covers the simulator confound, the routing-proxy upper bound, and the convention-mismatch caveat.

What remains is a thinner layer of consistency and framing defects. The most consequential: the definition of "frontier range" is internally inconsistent (GPT-5-mini at 59.92% sits in the comparison table labelled Frontier, while the abstract and the RQ3 viability threshold define the frontier range as starting at 71.00%); the abstract's parity claims with GPT-4.1 and Claude Sonnet 4.5 are cross-harness comparisons that §4.5 itself shows can diverge widely (B-template at 96.00% exceeds every frontier leaderboard score); and the abstract attributes "much of" the residual failure to schema information loss when the B-template control changes two variables at once (prompt format and schema detail) and cannot apportion between them. All are fixable in days, and one of them is precisely the experiment the thesis already names as future work (§6.3 p. 47).

**Indicative grade band (written thesis only, advisory): 10 (B).** The experimental design, the controls, the statistical treatment, and the honesty about negative and borderline results now comfortably exceed the typical MSc standard; the remaining weaknesses are "a few minor weaknesses" plus a small number of fixable consistency issues, which is the 10 descriptor. What would move it to 12: fix the frontier-set inconsistency (Issue 1), carry the harness caveat into the abstract's parity sentence (Issue 2), and either run the schema-enriched constrained-decoding experiment or soften the abstract's attribution to match §5.1's hedged wording (Issue 3). What would pull it back toward 7: leaving Issue 1 unfixed, since it sits inside the formal definition of RQ3's viability criterion.

## 3. Strengths

- **The B-template control is the best single addition.** It converts the thesis's largest vulnerability into its sharpest analytical instrument: it re-anchors RQ1 (p. 1, p. 44), bounds the information loss of the model-agnostic pipeline at 19.25 pp (§5.1 p. 36), disciplines the frontier comparison (§4.5 p. 31, where the thesis itself points out that 96.00% locally exceeding all leaderboard scores indicts cross-harness comparison, not frontier models), and produces a deployment guideline (benchmark the native template first, §6.2 p. 45).
- **Statistical reporting is now exemplary for an MSc.** Paired McNemar tests on stored per-case predictions (45/27, p = 0.044; 46/37, p = 0.38), interpreted against a measured ±0.5 pp run-to-run spread rather than at face value (§4.4.1 p. 28); the falsified determinism claim corrected in three places (§3.2.1, §4.2.1, §5.3.1); Wilson intervals quoted correctly (±4.1–4.4 pp, p. 31, which I re-verified); and an explicit refusal to rank τ-bench conditions within noise (§4.6.1 p. 32).
- **Honest, mechanistically analyzed negative results.** The CoT flip analysis (24 recovered vs. 51 broken, Fig. 4.2 p. 23) with verbatim reasoning traces (Table 4.2 p. 24); the RAG disambiguation analysis (recall@5 97.2% yet −24.5 pp, pp. 24–25); and the isolation table (Table 4.4 p. 26), which remains the strongest table in the thesis.
- **The format-alignment experiment is a genuine contribution.** Same data, same hyperparameters, training losses 0.203 vs. 0.229, yet 69.75% vs. 76.75% under constrained decoding (Fig. 4.4 p. 28), with the effect direction confirmed at all four model sizes (§4.7 p. 34).
- **The pipeline-boundary analysis is now coherent.** The parallel = 0% result is derived from the architecture (§3.5 p. 18, §5.3 p. 39), reconciled with the 1–4-call outputs on parallel multiple (§4.8.3 p. 35), and stated as a structural ceiling rather than a soft limitation.
- **Verified internal arithmetic.** I re-checked every delta and count I could derive from the document (Tables 4.1, 4.3–4.6, 4.8–4.11, C.1; all scaling gaps in §4.7 and §5.1.1; flip counts; τ-bench means; the 8.42 pp GPT-5-mini gap; the 19.25 pp B-template gap). With the exceptions in Issues 4 and 7 below, everything reconciles.
- **Reproducibility basics are in place:** repository link (p. 15), full hyperparameters (Appendix B), leaderboard snapshot date with mutability caveat (p. 30), and a clear generative-AI disclosure (Appendix A).

## 4. Evaluation by dimension

**Methodology & rigor.** The cumulative ladder with isolation controls remains fit for RQ2, and the two former methodological holes (baseline fairness, simulator confound) are now addressed: the first by the B-template control, the second by explicit acknowledgement in §3.2.2 (p. 16) and §5.3.1 (p. 40). The remaining methodological soft spot is interpretive rather than experimental: B-template differs from the constrained pipeline in two ways at once (native prompt format and full schema detail), so the repeated attribution of the residual to "schema information loss" (abstract; §5.1 p. 36; §6.1 p. 45) is a plausible hypothesis the current data cannot isolate. The thesis is aware of this (§5.1 says the 19.25 pp gap bounds "that information loss combined with the pipeline's prompt format") but the abstract states the attribution more strongly than the control supports. The direct test, enriching the constrained pipeline's prompts with the schema detail while keeping guided_json fixed, is named in §6.3 as a one-configuration experiment; running it would settle the thesis's main open interpretive question. The test-split-informed few-shot examples remain acknowledged (p. 19) and remain benign since the leakage favored the technique that lost.

**Technical & mathematical correctness.** Background math is correct. The new statistics are correctly computed: I verified both McNemar p-values to within rounding (45/27 gives p ≈ 0.044; 46/37 gives p ≈ 0.38) and the Wilson interval range. One bookkeeping defect: the quantized-pair counts do not reconcile with the headline accuracy unless the stored CD+Q run is 288/400 rather than the quoted 289/400 (289 + 46 − 37 = 298, but CD+Q+FT-aligned is reported as 297/400; §4.4.2 p. 28 vs. Table 4.5 p. 29). The full-precision pair is anchored explicitly ("the CD run scores 289/400", p. 28); the quantized pair is not, and a careful reader will do this subtraction. Smaller items: §3.1.2 (p. 14) says every job uses an A100 80 GB while §3.2.2 (p. 16) runs τ-bench on an L40S; and §3.2.2 states only CD is evaluated on τ-bench while Table 4.8 (p. 32) reports a CD+FT-aligned size sweep on τ-bench.

**Contribution & novelty.** Unchanged in substance and now better positioned: Tam et al. on format restrictions is engaged in §2.3.2 and §5.5 (pp. 7, 42), XGrammar and APIGen are cited (§2.3.2, §2.8.2), and the related-work chapter connects each of the thesis's four result clusters to its nearest prior work (§5.5 pp. 42–43). The contribution is honest application-and-synthesis work of the kind the cited survey [23] identifies as missing, with two elements of publishable interest: the format-alignment effect and the per-technique CD-marginal-contribution table. The B-template result sharpens rather than diminishes the contribution, since the thesis's value proposition is now correctly framed as structural guarantees at a quantified accuracy cost, not raw accuracy.

**Business & practical relevance.** The two former gaps are closed in form: production-viability is now an explicit two-condition criterion (§1.3 p. 3) answered within scope (§6.1 p. 45), and the unsupported "no additional inference cost" claim has been replaced by the accurate "without generating additional tokens" plus an explicit "latency was not measured" admission (§5.2 p. 37). Two residual quibbles. First, the viability threshold (71.00%, "the lowest frontier score in the comparison set") is inconsistent with the comparison set itself, which includes GPT-5-mini at 59.92% labelled Frontier (Table 4.6 p. 30); the criterion survives the fix, but as written the definition contradicts the thesis's own table. Second, §6.1 says "three configurations qualify" when plain CD (72.75%, 14.25 GiB) also literally meets both conditions; presumably it is subsumed by CD+Q, but the count should match the criterion. The escalation-rate framing of accuracy gains (§5.4 pp. 41–42) remains the right way to monetize the results, and the upper-bound caveat on p is now stated in both §5.3 and §5.3.1.

## 6. Questions for the defense

1. B-template changes two things at once relative to your pipeline: the prompt format matches the model's instruction tuning, and the full schema detail is visible. How do you apportion the 19.25 pp gap between those two factors, and why was the one-configuration experiment you describe in §6.3 (schema-enriched prompts under guided_json) not run?
2. Your local harness scores Qwen 2.5 7B under its native template at 96.00%, above every frontier score on the official leaderboard. Given that, what exactly does "on par with GPT-4.1" mean in your abstract, and would you defend that sentence if the official leaderboard contained a Qwen2.5-7B entry at a different number?
3. GPT-5-mini appears in your frontier comparison table at 59.92%, yet your production-viability threshold is "the lowest frontier score, 71.00%". Which models constitute the frontier set, and does CD at 0.5B (51.50%) fail or pass viability under your intended definition?
4. Your McNemar result for the full-precision pair is p = 0.044 on a stored CD run of 289/400, and you note a CD run at 291 would not reach significance. Given that the original per-case files behind the recorded 291 were overwritten, what is your best estimate of the probability that the format-alignment effect at 7B is real, and how much weight does the four-size consistency carry in that estimate?
5. Every residual failure after constrained decoding is structurally valid and therefore silent. Your viability criterion presumes escalation handles them, but no routing signal exists post-CD. What concrete semantic-routing mechanism would you deploy, and at what assumed routing accuracy does the cascade's economic argument break even?
6. You state latency was not measured. Guided decoding adds schema compilation and per-step masking; quantization halves memory but uses different kernels. For the deployment guidelines in §6.2 to be actionable, which configuration do you expect to have the highest per-request latency, and by roughly how much?

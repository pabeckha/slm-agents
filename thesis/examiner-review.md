# Examiner Review — Agents with Small Language Models: Evaluating Optimization Techniques for Reliable Tool Calling

**Author:** Paulo Ricardo Beckhauser de Araujo (s242779) · **Reviewed:** 2026-06-23 · **Pages:** 74 (printed body to p.65 + appendices)
**Reviewer note:** AI-assisted examiner review — to be verified and owned by the examiner. Page numbers are the thesis's *printed* page numbers unless marked "PDF".

## 1. Summary of the thesis

The thesis asks how far inference-time and lightweight training techniques can move a small language model (Qwen 2.5, 0.5B–7B) toward reliable tool calling, framed as widening the local tier of a cascade architecture. It runs a cumulative ablation (constrained decoding, schema enrichment, AWQ quantization, few-shot, chain-of-thought, RAG, LoRA) on BFCL v4 (primarily *simple python*, 400 cases) plus a multi-step τ-bench retail evaluation, a GitHub MCP real-world probe, and cross-family checks. The three research questions (p.4) are: **RQ1** the unoptimized SLM–frontier gap; **RQ2** the marginal contribution of each technique applied cumulatively; **RQ3** whether an optimized config can serve as an upper-bound local cascade tier on consumer hardware. The central finding is that constrained decoding converts tool calling from a *format-compliance* problem (structurally fixable) into a *semantic argument-value* problem, that schema-enriched constrained decoding (CD+schema, 89.00%) recovers most of the residual without training, and that none of this transfers to multi-turn agency (τ-bench 4.35%).

## 2. Overall assessment

This is a strong, mature, and unusually honest empirical thesis. Its defining quality is intellectual integrity: the author repeatedly builds the strongest version of the counter-argument to their own headline. The B-template control (96.00%, p.25) shows that the same model, prompted through its native template, already solves the task and *beats every constrained configuration* — and the thesis says so plainly (p.51, p.57). It leads with the conservative same-parser gain (10.75 pp) rather than the dramatic strict-parser recovery (71.25 pp), quantifies run-to-run spread (±0.5 pp, p.50), flags a borderline p-value as borderline (p = 0.044, p.33), and labels a non-significant result as noise (p = 0.38, p.33). The format/semantic decomposition is a genuinely clean organizing idea, CD+schema (89.00%, McNemar p < 0.00001, p.27) is a real and well-supported result, and the parallel-category "0% by construction → 79% under a joint schema" analysis (p.43) is exactly the kind of mechanism-level insight that distinguishes good empirical work. Internal arithmetic is consistent throughout (I verified the McNemar contingencies, the flip analyses, and every accuracy fraction I checked).

What holds it back from the top of the scale is not correctness but *framing and comparability*. The contribution narrative is anchored to a deliberately weak baseline; the title ("Agents") oversells a thesis whose agentic result is essentially negative; the frontier comparisons that carry RQ3 are cross-harness; and several headline numbers are honest only if the reader finds a table footnote. None of these is a wrong result — they are presentation and scope decisions a censor will press on. **Indicative grade band (written thesis only): 10 (B), with a credible path to 12 (A).** What moves it up: tighten the contribution/abstract framing so the genuine contribution (model-agnostic structural guarantee + CD+schema + the multi-call boundary) is what the reader takes away rather than the 1.5%→89% optics; re-ground the small-size strict baselines; and soften the cross-harness "above GPT-5-mini" claim to the parity it actually supports. What would pull it down: if the defense reveals the contribution collapses to "use the native template" without a convincing model-agnostic-deployment story.

## 3. Strengths

- **Self-undercutting honesty as method.** The B-template control (96.00%, Table 4.1, p.25) is allowed to beat the author's own pipeline, and the thesis reinterprets the headline gap as "the cost of naive integration rather than a capability deficit" (p.45, p.50). This is rare and valuable.
- **Parser transparency.** The 1.5% strict floor vs. 62.00% lenient first-object parser distinction is made explicit (Section 3.4, p.20; Table 4.5, p.31), and the conservative 10.75 pp gain is what the abstract and contributions foreground.
- **Statistical discipline.** Paired McNemar on stored per-case predictions (Table 4.2, p.27), Wilson CIs (±4.1–4.4 pp, p.35), explicit run-to-run spread, and refusal to over-read a 0.5 pp delta (p.50).
- **A real no-training result.** CD+schema 356/400 = 89.00%, +16.25 pp over CD, p < 0.00001 (p.27) — the strongest configuration, with a clean mechanism (restoring discarded schema vocabulary).
- **Mechanism over metric.** The CoT flip analysis (24 gained, 51 broken, Fig. 4.2, p.28) and the parallel single-vs-joint-schema explanation (p.43) diagnose *why*, not just *how much*.
- **Breadth that supports the central claim.** Cross-family CD (Table 4.14, p.41), full size sweep (Table 4.13, p.40), extended BFCL categories (Section 4.9), and a live GitHub MCP probe with real REST execution (Table 4.11, p.39).
- **Reproducibility and ethics.** Hardware/software stack (p.16), hyperparameter appendix (Table B.1, p.64), public repo, and a clear generative-AI disclosure (Appendix A, p.63).

## 4. Evaluation by dimension

**Methodology & rigor.** Strong and fit for RQ1–RQ2. The cumulative-ladder-plus-isolation-arm design (p.16) cleanly separates each technique's standalone effect from constrained decoding's contribution. The main weaknesses are acknowledged: PE few-shot examples were designed by inspecting test-split errors (p.22, p.50) — a genuine contamination, though the author's "bias is in PE's favour, so the negative result is conservative" argument (p.50) is logically sound and largely neutralizes it. RAG (CD+Q+RAG) changes the *task* (open 5-candidate catalogue vs. oracle single candidate), so its −24.5 pp sits in the ladder table (Table 4.7, p.33) alongside same-task configs even though the text is explicit it is not a like-for-like ablation (p.29). Most BFCL cells are single runs (temperature 0, deterministic within an environment); the author defends this with the ±0.5 pp spread and leans on cross-size consistency rather than single p-values — defensible. RQ3 is the softest: no cascade router is built, and the cost model (Section 5.4) is candid that the *p* values are upper bounds under an ideal semantic router (p.51).

**Technical & mathematical correctness.** I found no incorrect results. Every fraction and contingency I checked reconciles: CD+schema 283+73 = 356 and the paired CD 283+7 = 290 (Table 4.2); CD+FT-aligned 289+45−27 = 307 (p.33); CoT 238+24 = 262 (Fig. 4.2); RAG 400−(128+8+73) = 191 (p.30). One terminology imprecision: merging the LoRA adapter is described as "linear interpolation" (p.23, p.32), but the operation is the additive low-rank update W + BA defined correctly on p.11 — "interpolation" is the wrong word for it. The attention/GQA/AWQ background (Sections 2.1, 2.7) is standard and correct.

**Contribution & novelty.** The honest one-sentence contribution is: *constrained decoding provides a model-agnostic structural guarantee for tool-call generation, and schema enrichment recovers most of the residual semantic gap without training.* That is solid, useful, and appropriately scoped for an MSc — but narrower than the framing implies, and the thesis's own B-template result is the reason. The most novel pieces are the format/semantic decomposition, the output-format-alignment effect on LoRA (two adapters, identical loss, 7 pp apart, p.33), and the single-vs-joint-schema boundary on *parallel* (p.43). Related-work coverage is good and current (Gorilla, xLAM/APIGen, ToolLLM, Outlines/XGrammar, Tam et al. on format restrictions), and prior approaches are represented fairly.

**Business / practical relevance.** Treated as a deployment cost argument rather than a market study, which is the right register. The cascade unit economics (break-even N = C_train/(Δp·c_frontier), p.52) are transparent about everything they omit (router, utilization, energy, maintenance). The honesty that, for a single frozen Qwen deployment, the native template is the *better* local baseline (p.51, p.57) is commendable but also quietly concedes that the practical pitch rests entirely on the multi-model / model-agnostic scenario — which is asserted, not demonstrated with a heterogeneous-fleet experiment.

## 6. Questions for the defense

1. Your B-template control reaches 96% and beats every constrained configuration. In one sentence, what is the contribution of this thesis to a team that deploys a single Qwen 2.5 7B model — and is there any experiment here that demonstrates (not asserts) the model-agnostic-fleet benefit?
2. The "CD+schema is above GPT-5-mini" claim compares your local 89% to a leaderboard 78.75%. What would you expect if GPT-5-mini were re-run under your exact harness, and how much of the 10.25 pp could be harness difference?
3. RAG drops 24.5 pp, but it is the only configuration evaluated on an open catalogue rather than an oracle single candidate. Is the comparison to CD+Q meaningful at all, and what is the right baseline for measuring "the cost of retrieval"?
4. CD+FT-aligned is significant at p = 0.044 — which you yourself say is within run-to-run spread. On what evidence do you claim format-aligned fine-tuning "helps," and would you defend that claim if the CD reference had scored 291 instead of 289?
5. The PE few-shot examples were tuned to errors observed on the test split. Walk me through why this does not invalidate the PE result, and what a clean measurement would have required.
6. τ-bench is 4.35% and does not move with scale or fine-tuning. Given the title is "Agents," why should this thesis not be read primarily as a negative result about small-model agency?
